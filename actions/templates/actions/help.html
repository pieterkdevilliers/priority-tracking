Using Priority Tracker

Overview

Priority Tracker was created to help you to identify how much of your time is spent working on your priorities, and how mush time is spent on distractions. This is not intended as a todo-list to plan what you want to do, but to track what you are actually working on, and where your time is going.

You'll be able to track your time against tasks, and allocate those tasks under Categories and Priorities. Priority Tracker will then show you what percentage of your time is spent On-Priority and what is spent Off-Priority.

Categories, Priorities and Actions

Categories

Categories are the big rocks. Think of these as departments. Example Categories might be Finance, Admin, Design. Categories are never finished. You must add your Categories before adding your Priorities, in order to group your Priorities under Categories.

Priorities

Priorities are the things you want to get done, within a specific Category. For example, looking at the Categories above, you could have respective Priorities of January Payroll, Staff Handbook Update, Website Refresh. Each of these fall within a Category, but will at some piont be finished. Priorities should be created after Categories, in order to group Priorities uunder Categories.

Actions

Actions are the things you actually DO. You track your time against individual Actions. Because these Actions are grouped under your Priorities, you can easily see what proportion of time is spent on Off-Priority work. Creating and tracking Actions without a Priority will cause your Off-Priority percentage to increase.
